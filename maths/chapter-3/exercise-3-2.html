Substitution Method — How it works:

  • Step 1: From one equation, express one variable in terms of the other.
  • Step 2: Substitute this value in the second equation.
  • Step 3: Solve to get one variable.
  • Step 4: Substitute back to get the other variable.
  • Step 5: Verify by substituting both values in original equations.

Question 1

Question:

Solve the following pair of linear equations by the substitution method:

(i) x + y = 14 and x – y = 4

(ii) s – t = 3 and s/3 + t/2 = 6

(iii) 3x – y = 3 and 9x – 3y = 9

(iv) 0.2x + 0.3y = 1.3 and 0.4x + 0.5y = 2.3

(v) √2 x + √3 y = 0 and √3 x – √8 y = 0

(vi) 3x/2 – 5y/3 = –2 and x/3 + y/2 = 13/6

Solution (i): x + y = 14 and x – y = 4

Step 1: From equation (1): x + y = 14

x = 14 – y ... (A)

Step 2: Substitute (A) in equation (2): x – y = 4

(14 – y) – y = 4

14 – 2y = 4

2y = 10

y = 5

Step 3: Put y = 5 in (A):

x = 14 – 5 = 9

Step 4: Verify:

x + y = 9 + 5 = 14 ✅

x – y = 9 – 5 = 4 ✅

Answer: x = 9, y = 5

Solution (ii): s – t = 3 and s/3 + t/2 = 6

Step 1: From equation (1): s = t + 3 ... (A)

Step 2: Substitute in equation (2):

(t + 3)/3 + t/2 = 6

Multiply throughout by 6:

2(t + 3) + 3t = 36

2t + 6 + 3t = 36

5t = 30

t = 6

Step 3: s = 6 + 3 = 9

Verify: s – t = 9 – 6 = 3 ✅ and 9/3 + 6/2 = 3 + 3 = 6 ✅

Answer: s = 9, t = 6

Solution (iii): 3x – y = 3 and 9x – 3y = 9

Step 1: From equation (1): y = 3x – 3 ... (A)

Step 2: Substitute in equation (2):

9x – 3(3x – 3) = 9

9x – 9x + 9 = 9

9 = 9

This is always true — it is an identity!

Answer: The equations have infinitely many solutions. Lines are coincident.

General solution: y = 3x – 3, where x can be any real number.

Solution (iv): 0.2x + 0.3y = 1.3 and 0.4x + 0.5y = 2.3

Step 1: Multiply both equations by 10 to remove decimals.

2x + 3y = 13 ... (1)

4x + 5y = 23 ... (2)

Step 2: From (1): x = (13 – 3y)/2 ... (A)

Step 3: Substitute in (2):

4 × (13 – 3y)/2 + 5y = 23

2(13 – 3y) + 5y = 23

26 – 6y + 5y = 23

–y = –3

y = 3

Step 4: x = (13 – 9)/2 = 4/2 = 2

Verify: 0.2(2) + 0.3(3) = 0.4 + 0.9 = 1.3 ✅

0.4(2) + 0.5(3) = 0.8 + 1.5 = 2.3 ✅

Answer: x = 2, y = 3

Solution (v): √2 x + √3 y = 0 and √3 x – √8 y = 0

Step 1: From equation (1): √2 x = –√3 y

x = –√3 y / √2 ... (A)

Step 2: Substitute in equation (2):

√3 × (–√3 y/√2) – √8 y = 0

–3y/√2 – 2√2 y = 0

Multiply by √2:

–3y – 4y = 0

–7y = 0

y = 0

Step 3: x = –√3(0)/√2 = 0

Answer: x = 0, y = 0

Solution (vi): 3x/2 – 5y/3 = –2 and x/3 + y/2 = 13/6

Step 1: Multiply equation (1) by 6:

9x – 10y = –12 ... (1)

Step 2: Multiply equation (2) by 6:

2x + 3y = 13 ... (2)

Step 3: From (2): x = (13 – 3y)/2 ... (A)

Step 4: Substitute in (1):

9(13 – 3y)/2 – 10y = –12

Multiply by 2:

9(13 – 3y) – 20y = –24

117 – 27y – 20y = –24

–47y = –141

y = 3

Step 5: x = (13 – 9)/2 = 2

Verify: 3(2)/2 – 5(3)/3 = 3 – 5 = –2 ✅

2/3 + 3/2 = 4/6 + 9/6 = 13/6 ✅

Answer: x = 2, y = 3

Question 2

Question:

Solve the following questions by substitution method:

(i) The difference between two numbers is 26 and one number is three times the other. Find them.

(ii) The larger of two supplementary angles exceeds the smaller by 18 degrees. Find them.

(iii) The coach of a cricket team buys 7 bats and 6 balls for Rs 3800. Later, she buys 3 bats and 5 balls for Rs 1750. Find the cost of each bat and each ball.

(iv) The taxi charges in a city consist of a fixed charge together with the charge for the distance covered. For a distance of 10 km, the charge paid is Rs 105 and for a journey of 15 km, the charge paid is Rs 155. What are the fixed charges and the charge per km?

(v) A fraction becomes 9/11, if 2 is added to both the numerator and the denominator. If 3 is added to both the numerator and the denominator it becomes 5/6. Find the fraction.

(vi) Five years hence, the age of Jacob will be three times that of his son. Five years ago, Jacob's age was seven times that of his son. What are their present ages?

Solution (i): Difference is 26, one number is three times other

Let numbers be x and y. x = 3y and x – y = 26

Substitute x = 3y: 3y – y = 26 → 2y = 26 → y = 13

x = 3 × 13 = 39

Answer: The two numbers are 39 and 13.

Solution (ii): Supplementary angles, larger exceeds smaller by 18°

Let angles be x and y. x + y = 180 and x – y = 18

From (2): x = y + 18

Substitute: (y + 18) + y = 180 → 2y = 162 → y = 81°

x = 81 + 18 = 99°

Answer: The angles are 99° and 81°.

Solution (iii): 7 bats + 6 balls = Rs 3800, 3 bats + 5 balls = Rs 1750

Let cost of bat = Rs x, ball = Rs y

7x + 6y = 3800 ... (1)

3x + 5y = 1750 ... (2)

From (2): x = (1750 – 5y)/3 ... (A)

Substitute in (1): 7(1750 – 5y)/3 + 6y = 3800

7(1750 – 5y) + 18y = 11400

12250 – 35y + 18y = 11400

–17y = –850 → y = 50

x = (1750 – 250)/3 = 1500/3 = 500

Answer: Cost of bat = Rs 500, Cost of ball = Rs 50.

Solution (iv): Taxi fixed charge + per km charge

Let fixed charge = Rs x, charge per km = Rs y

x + 10y = 105 ... (1)

x + 15y = 155 ... (2)

From (1): x = 105 – 10y ... (A)

Substitute in (2): (105 – 10y) + 15y = 155

5y = 50 → y = 10

x = 105 – 100 = 5

Answer: Fixed charge = Rs 5, Charge per km = Rs 10.

Solution (v): Fraction becomes 9/11 when 2 added, 5/6 when 3 added

Let fraction = x/y

(x + 2)/(y + 2) = 9/11 → 11x + 22 = 9y + 18 → 11x – 9y = –4 ... (1)

(x + 3)/(y + 3) = 5/6 → 6x + 18 = 5y + 15 → 6x – 5y = –3 ... (2)

From (2): x = (5y – 3)/6 ... (A)

Substitute in (1): 11(5y – 3)/6 – 9y = –4

11(5y – 3) – 54y = –24

55y – 33 – 54y = –24

y = 9

x = (45 – 3)/6 = 42/6 = 7

Answer: The fraction is 7/9.

Solution (vi): Jacob and son age problem

Let Jacob's present age = x, son's present age = y

Five years hence: x + 5 = 3(y + 5) → x – 3y = 10 ... (1)

Five years ago: x – 5 = 7(y – 5) → x – 7y = –30 ... (2)

From (1): x = 3y + 10 ... (A)

Substitute in (2): (3y + 10) – 7y = –30

–4y = –40 → y = 10

x = 3(10) + 10 = 40

Answer: Jacob's present age = 40 years, Son's present age = 10 years.

Key Points – Exercise 3.2

  • Substitution method works best when one variable has coefficient 1.
  • Always verify answers in both original equations.
  • If result is always true (like 9 = 9) → infinite solutions.
  • If result is false (like 9 = 0) → no solution.
  • Remove fractions by multiplying by LCM before solving.
