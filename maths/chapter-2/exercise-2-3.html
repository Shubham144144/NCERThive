Division Algorithm for Polynomials:

If p(x) and g(x) are two polynomials where g(x) ≠ 0, then:

p(x) = g(x) × q(x) + r(x)

Where q(x) is the quotient and r(x) is the remainder.

Degree of r(x) is always less than degree of g(x).

Question 1

Question:

Divide the polynomial p(x) by the polynomial g(x) and find the quotient and remainder in each case:

(i) p(x) = x³ – 3x² + 5x – 3, g(x) = x² – 2

(ii) p(x) = x⁴ – 3x² + 4x + 5, g(x) = x² + 1 – x

(iii) p(x) = x⁴ – 5x + 6, g(x) = 2 – x²

Solution (i): p(x) = x³ – 3x² + 5x – 3, g(x) = x² – 2

Step 1: Divide x³ by x² → quotient term = x

Multiply: x × (x² – 2) = x³ – 2x

Subtract: (x³ – 3x² + 5x – 3) – (x³ – 2x) = –3x² + 7x – 3

Step 2: Divide –3x² by x² → quotient term = –3

Multiply: –3 × (x² – 2) = –3x² + 6

Subtract: (–3x² + 7x – 3) – (–3x² + 6) = 7x – 9

Step 3: Degree of (7x – 9) is 1, which is less than degree of g(x) = 2. Stop.

Answer:

Quotient q(x) = x – 3

Remainder r(x) = 7x – 9

Verify: g(x) × q(x) + r(x) = (x² – 2)(x – 3) + (7x – 9)

= x³ – 3x² – 2x + 6 + 7x – 9 = x³ – 3x² + 5x – 3 = p(x) ✅

Solution (ii): p(x) = x⁴ – 3x² + 4x + 5, g(x) = x² – x + 1

Step 1: Divide x⁴ by x² → quotient term = x²

Multiply: x² × (x² – x + 1) = x⁴ – x³ + x²

Subtract: (x⁴ – 3x² + 4x + 5) – (x⁴ – x³ + x²) = x³ – 4x² + 4x + 5

Step 2: Divide x³ by x² → quotient term = x

Multiply: x × (x² – x + 1) = x³ – x² + x

Subtract: (x³ – 4x² + 4x + 5) – (x³ – x² + x) = –3x² + 3x + 5

Step 3: Divide –3x² by x² → quotient term = –3

Multiply: –3 × (x² – x + 1) = –3x² + 3x – 3

Subtract: (–3x² + 3x + 5) – (–3x² + 3x – 3) = 8

Answer:

Quotient q(x) = x² + x – 3

Remainder r(x) = 8

Solution (iii): p(x) = x⁴ – 5x + 6, g(x) = –x² + 2

Step 1: Rewrite g(x) = –x² + 2. Divide x⁴ by –x² → quotient term = –x²

Multiply: –x² × (–x² + 2) = x⁴ – 2x²

Subtract: (x⁴ – 5x + 6) – (x⁴ – 2x²) = 2x² – 5x + 6

Step 2: Divide 2x² by –x² → quotient term = –2

Multiply: –2 × (–x² + 2) = 2x² – 4

Subtract: (2x² – 5x + 6) – (2x² – 4) = –5x + 10

Answer:

Quotient q(x) = –x² – 2

Remainder r(x) = –5x + 10

Question 2

Question:

Check whether the first polynomial is a factor of the second polynomial by dividing the second polynomial by the first polynomial:

(i) t² – 3, 2t⁴ + 3t³ – 2t² – 9t – 12

(ii) x² + 3x + 1, 3x⁴ + 5x³ – 7x² + 2x + 2

(iii) x³ – 3x + 1, x⁵ – 4x³ + x² + 3x + 1

Solution (i): t² – 3 and 2t⁴ + 3t³ – 2t² – 9t – 12

Step 1: Divide 2t⁴ by t² → 2t²

2t² × (t² – 3) = 2t⁴ – 6t²

Subtract: 3t³ + 4t² – 9t – 12

Step 2: Divide 3t³ by t² → 3t

3t × (t² – 3) = 3t³ – 9t

Subtract: 4t² – 12

Step 3: Divide 4t² by t² → 4

4 × (t² – 3) = 4t² – 12

Subtract: 0

Remainder = 0

Answer: t² – 3 IS a factor of 2t⁴ + 3t³ – 2t² – 9t – 12. ✅

Solution (ii): x² + 3x + 1 and 3x⁴ + 5x³ – 7x² + 2x + 2

Step 1: Divide 3x⁴ by x² → 3x²

3x² × (x² + 3x + 1) = 3x⁴ + 9x³ + 3x²

Subtract: –4x³ – 10x² + 2x + 2

Step 2: Divide –4x³ by x² → –4x

–4x × (x² + 3x + 1) = –4x³ – 12x² – 4x

Subtract: 2x² + 6x + 2

Step 3: Divide 2x² by x² → 2

2 × (x² + 3x + 1) = 2x² + 6x + 2

Subtract: 0

Remainder = 0

Answer: x² + 3x + 1 IS a factor of 3x⁴ + 5x³ – 7x² + 2x + 2. ✅

Solution (iii): x³ – 3x + 1 and x⁵ – 4x³ + x² + 3x + 1

Step 1: Divide x⁵ by x³ → x²

x² × (x³ – 3x + 1) = x⁵ – 3x³ + x²

Subtract: –x³ + 3x + 1

Step 2: Divide –x³ by x³ → –1

–1 × (x³ – 3x + 1) = –x³ + 3x – 1

Subtract: 2

Remainder = 2 ≠ 0

Answer: x³ – 3x + 1 is NOT a factor of x⁵ – 4x³ + x² + 3x + 1. ❌

Question 3

Question:

Obtain all other zeroes of 3x⁴ + 6x³ – 2x² – 10x – 5, if two of its zeroes are √(5/3) and –√(5/3).

Solution:

Step 1: Since √(5/3) and –√(5/3) are zeroes:

(x – √(5/3))(x + √(5/3)) = x² – 5/3 is a factor.

Multiply by 3: 3x² – 5 is a factor.

Step 2: Divide 3x⁴ + 6x³ – 2x² – 10x – 5 by 3x² – 5.

Divide 3x⁴ by 3x² → x²

x² × (3x² – 5) = 3x⁴ – 5x²

Subtract: 6x³ + 3x² – 10x – 5

Step 3: Divide 6x³ by 3x² → 2x

2x × (3x² – 5) = 6x³ – 10x

Subtract: 3x² – 5

Step 4: Divide 3x² by 3x² → 1

1 × (3x² – 5) = 3x² – 5

Subtract: 0

Quotient = x² + 2x + 1 = (x + 1)²

Step 5: Find zeroes of (x + 1)²

x + 1 = 0 → x = –1 (repeated zero)

Answer: All four zeroes are √(5/3), –√(5/3), –1, and –1.

Question 4

Question:

On dividing x³ – 3x² + x + 2 by a polynomial g(x), the quotient and remainder were x – 2 and –2x + 4, respectively. Find g(x).

Solution:

Step 1: Use Division Algorithm:

p(x) = g(x) × q(x) + r(x)

x³ – 3x² + x + 2 = g(x) × (x – 2) + (–2x + 4)

Step 2: Rearrange to find g(x).

g(x) × (x – 2) = x³ – 3x² + x + 2 – (–2x + 4)

g(x) × (x – 2) = x³ – 3x² + 3x – 2

Step 3: Divide x³ – 3x² + 3x – 2 by (x – 2).

Divide x³ by x → x²

x² × (x – 2) = x³ – 2x²

Subtract: –x² + 3x – 2

Divide –x² by x → –x

–x × (x – 2) = –x² + 2x

Subtract: x – 2

Divide x by x → 1

1 × (x – 2) = x – 2

Subtract: 0

Answer: g(x) = x² – x + 1

Question 5

Question:

Give examples of polynomials p(x), g(x), q(x) and r(x), which satisfy the division algorithm and:

(i) deg p(x) = deg q(x)    (ii) deg q(x) = deg r(x)    (iii) deg r(x) = 0

Solution:

(i) deg p(x) = deg q(x)

This happens when g(x) is a constant polynomial (degree 0).

Example: p(x) = 2x² + 2x + 6, g(x) = 2

q(x) = x² + x + 3, r(x) = 0

Check: deg p(x) = 2 = deg q(x) = 2 ✅

(ii) deg q(x) = deg r(x)

Example: p(x) = x³ + x², g(x) = x² – 1

q(x) = x + 1, r(x) = x + 1

Check: deg q(x) = 1 = deg r(x) = 1 ✅

(iii) deg r(x) = 0

This means remainder is a constant (non-zero number).

Example: p(x) = x³ + 1, g(x) = x²

q(x) = x, r(x) = 1

Check: deg r(x) = 0 ✅

Key Points – Exercise 2.3

  • Division Algorithm: p(x) = g(x) × q(x) + r(x)
  • Degree of remainder is always less than degree of divisor.
  • If remainder = 0, then divisor is a factor of dividend.
  • Always arrange polynomials in descending order of degree before dividing.
  • Insert 0 as coefficient for missing terms (e.g. x⁴ + x² write as x⁴ + 0x³ + x²).
