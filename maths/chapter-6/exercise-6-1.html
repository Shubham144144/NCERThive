Class 10 Maths Chapter 6 Exercise 6.1 Solutions 2026-27 English Medium

Key Concepts:

  • Similar figures: Same shape but different size. All corresponding angles are equal and corresponding sides are in proportion.
  • Congruent figures: Same shape AND same size.
  • All congruent figures are similar but all similar figures are NOT congruent.
  • All circles are similar. All squares are similar. All equilateral triangles are similar.

Question 1

Fill in the blanks using the correct word given in the brackets:

(i) All circles are ___________. (congruent, similar)

Solution:

All circles have the same shape but can have different sizes (different radii).

Answer: All circles are similar.

(ii) All squares are ___________. (similar, congruent)

Solution:

All squares have the same shape (all angles = 90°) but can have different sizes.

Answer: All squares are similar.

(iii) All ___________ triangles are similar. (isosceles, equilateral)

Solution:

All equilateral triangles have all angles = 60°. So they are always similar to each other.

Answer: All equilateral triangles are similar.

(iv) Two polygons of the same number of sides are similar, if (a) their corresponding angles are ___________ and (b) their corresponding sides are ___________. (equal, proportional)

Solution:

(a) Corresponding angles must be equal.

(b) Corresponding sides must be proportional.

Answer: (a) equal, (b) proportional

Question 2

Give two different examples of pair of:

(i) Similar figures

Solution:

Example 1: Two circles with different radii — say radius 3 cm and radius 5 cm.

Example 2: Two equilateral triangles with sides 4 cm and 8 cm.

Answer: (1) Two circles of radii 3 cm and 5 cm. (2) Two equilateral triangles of sides 4 cm and 8 cm.

(ii) Non-similar figures

Solution:

Example 1: A circle and a square — different shapes, so not similar.

Example 2: An isosceles triangle and a scalene triangle — different shapes, so not similar.

Answer: (1) A circle and a square. (2) An isosceles triangle and a scalene triangle.

Question 3

State whether the following quadrilaterals are similar or not:

Solution:

Step 1: Check if corresponding angles are equal.

Figure 1 is a rectangle — all angles = 90°.

Figure 2 is a rhombus — angles are NOT all 90°.

Step 2: Even though sides may be proportional, corresponding angles are NOT equal.

For similarity, BOTH conditions must be satisfied — equal angles AND proportional sides.

Answer: The two quadrilaterals are NOT similar.

(A rectangle and a rhombus have sides in proportion but angles are different, so they are not similar.)

Key Points – Exercise 6.1

  • Similar figures: same shape, different size.
  • Congruent figures: same shape, same size.
  • For similarity: corresponding angles must be equal AND corresponding sides proportional.
  • Both conditions are necessary — one alone is not enough.
  • All circles, all squares, all equilateral triangles are similar to each other.
