Exercise 6.1 Solutions

Question 1

Fill in the blanks using the correct word given in brackets:

(i) All circles are _______. (congruent, similar)

(ii) All squares are _______. (similar, congruent)

(iii) All _______ triangles are similar. (isosceles, equilateral)

(iv) Two polygons of the same number of sides are similar, if (a) their corresponding angles are _______ and (b) their corresponding sides are _______. (equal, proportional)

Solution

(i) All circles are similar.

(ii) All squares are similar.

(iii) All equilateral triangles are similar.

(iv) Two polygons of the same number of sides are similar, if (a) their corresponding angles are equal and (b) their corresponding sides are proportional.

Question 2

Give two different examples of pair of

(i) similar figures.

(ii) non-similar figures.

Solution

(i) Similar figures:

Example 1: Any two circles are similar figures, whatever be their radii, since they always have the same shape.

Example 2: Any two squares are similar figures, whatever be the length of their sides, since all angles are 90 degrees in both and all sides are in the same ratio.

(ii) Non-similar figures:

Example 1: A circle and a square are non-similar figures, since a circle has no straight sides while a square has four straight sides.

Example 2: An equilateral triangle and a scalene triangle are non-similar figures, since their corresponding angles are not equal.
